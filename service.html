Education

Next we are interested in understand the education level in Europe. There are many ways to conduct researches in order to measure a country's education rate. For our project we used a dataset which relies on PISA assessment. The Program for International Student Assessment (PISA) assessment, is coordinated by the OECD and is the most well known international assessment of learning outcomes. The first PISA study was carried out in 1997 and since then it was repeated every three years. We took the latest PISA score in reading literacy as a scale to measure education rates within the European nations.

Here is the heatmap of the dataset below:

Download dataset

It comes to our attention that countries like Norway, Finland, Sweden etc show a higher score, indicating that northern European states have a slightly higher PISA score rate which can be interpreted as northern Europe having a slightly higher education score as well.


Internet use vs education

Now we will combine the two datasets we have to get a general understanding of the relation between percentage of internet users and education score (PISA score) of the European states.

Here is the scatterplot of the two datasets:

Download dataset

We can infer from the graph above a clear relation between the two variables. We see a postive relation between PISA mean score and percentage of intenet users. Countries like Romania, Bulgaria, Greece etc. show a low PISA score and also a low percentage of internet users. Consequently, countries like Germany, Finland, Ireland etc show a high percentage of internet users and a high PISA score.

This result can be interpreted in a way where we infer some relationship between internet users and education. We could very well say from the graph above that accordingly to our data, higher use of internet leads to a higher education rate.

Internet use vs education vs unemployment

Finally we can move towards our final question. To see the relation between intenet, education and unemployment.

Download dataset

We had already concluded that there is some relation between education and internet user percentage. Now once we plug in unemployment rate we get the full picture.

We see that at Countries like Sweden, Spain, Latvia etc. have higher PISA score but also higher unemployment rate. However, there are counties like Finland, Slovania, Germany, Poland etc. who have a relatively high PISA score but they have lower unemployment rate.

This goes to show that unemployment rate is not dependent on education score according to the data we have. Similarly, there is no such pattern of any relation between percentage of internet users and unemployment rate as well because no such relation can be detected from the visualizations from the data we used.
