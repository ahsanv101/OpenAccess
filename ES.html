Quality Analysis


The standards for quality we decided to take into consideration are those of the Open Data Goldbook for Data Managers and Data Holders (provided by European data portal), which are completeness (high level of indicators), cleanness (no empty fields or double entries), accuracy (data fit for its purpose), timeliness (data maintained, up-to-date) and consistency (equal quality of datasets).

While the completeness of the data depends on the availability of data from the national statistical institutes, there is a high level of indicators for the data, which we can select specifically according to our research needs. Fields of which the value is not available is marked by a semicolon, and those which are estimated or provisional are respectively marked by an ‘e’ or a ‘p’. The dataset is made accurate through the performance of a data validation, that checks “whether data are in accordance with certain basic criteria that serve to assess the plausibility of the given data” - that is, “an activity verifying whether or not a combination of values is a member of a set of acceptable combinations”. The data is updated through the years.

Legal Analysis


The datasets we collected derive from different sources that use different licenses. Along with considering the license used by each of our sources, we considered general transparency of the data and the authorities liable for it. These legal aspects come hand in hand with certain purposes that are assigned to each of our datasets.

The license used is Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0). The European Data Protection Supervisor “acts as an independent supervisory authority” and is liable to the privacy matters for the data.

The Creative Commons license goes hand in hand with Eurostat’s policy of encouraging free re-use of its data, for both non-commercial and commercial-purposes - provided that the source is indicated as Eurostat and that modifications to the data or text is clearly stated to the end user of the information.

Ethical Analysis


Data ethics is about the responsible and sustainable use of data: is it right for individuals and society? Its main principles concern human rights and privacy protection, but also honest and transparent use of the data. We thus verified if our different datasets were created in a “privacy-by-design” approach, in the entirety of its process (with a particular attention to anonymization), and checked if our datasets are free from discrimination, cognitive bias and prejudice.

As transparently stated in Eurostat’s “privacy policy” page, there is a “Data Protection Officer” working with Eurostat, which “ensures, in an independent way, that the Commission correctly applies the law concerning individuals’ personal data and keeps a public register of all operations carried out by the Commission involving personal data”. Cooperating with the European Data Protection Supervisor, it applies internally the EU regulation 2018/1725.

There is no cognitive bias or prejudice in the data, thanks to a particular attention to the methodological aspects of their data collection and dissemination. In the explanatory texts that come with each datasets, it is stated that regulation of the European Commission No 223/2009 on European Statistics “stipulates the need to establish common principles and guidelines ensuring the confidentiality of data used for the production of European statistics and the access to those confidential data with due account for technical developments and the requirements of users in a democratic society” - a confidentiality policy which they apply carefully to the data they collect and display.

Technical Analysis


  • Format: xlxs, SDMX 2.1, tsv, csv, json
  • Metadata: Eurostat provides a considerable amount of metadata on its datasets, expressed in SDMX format, an ISO standard since 2013 for the exchange of statistical data and metadata in XML format. These metadata contain information on contacts, on the updating of the metadata themselves, on the presentation of data, on the units of measurement, on the reference period, on the institutional mandate, on confidentiality, on the distribution policy, on the frequency of dissemination, on accessibility, on quality, on relevance, on accuracy, on consistency, on cost, on review and other comments.
